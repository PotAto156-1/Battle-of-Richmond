Interesting Facts

Smith’s forces used two hidden ravines to conceal flanking maneuvers: Cleburne attacked the Union left, while Churchill swung around to crush their right—the terrain tricked the Union commanders Troops fought in nearly 100°F heat and dry, dusty terrain. The weather and thick dust intensified the panic among inexperienced Union soldiers The Confederate victory cleared the road for Smith to capture Frankfort on September 2—the only Union state capital ever taken by the Confederacy.
