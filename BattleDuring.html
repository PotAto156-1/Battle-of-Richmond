During the Battle

On August 29–30, 1862, Union forces under Gen. Mahlon D. Manson clashed with Confederate troops near Richmond, Kentucky. After initial success, Manson’s men were stopped by a stronger Confederate line under Gen. Patrick Cleburne. The next day, Confederate forces, including Gen. Thomas Churchill’s division, launched a major assault near Mt. Zion Church. Despite reinforcements from Gen. Charles Cruft, the Union lines collapsed, and the soldiers retreated in confusion. Gen. William “Bull” Nelson tried to regroup the troops near Richmond but was forced to retreat as Confederate attacks continued. Most Union soldiers were captured during the chaotic retreat, though Nelson escaped despite being wounded.
