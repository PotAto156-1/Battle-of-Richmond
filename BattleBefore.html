Before the Battle

Many Midwest states such as Kentuck began to fear large-scale Confederate invasions, so Midwest governors created regiments with little or no training and sent them to the front line. These were the men who stood between Richmond and Brigadier General E.Kirby Smith and his army. General Charles Cruft and Mahlon D. Manson controled 2 newly formed brigades centered around Richmond. By August 28th, the Confederate army, led by division commander General Patrick R. Cleburne, neared Richmond, Kentucky. With both armies in position, the first large-scale fight of the Kentucky Campaign had begun.
