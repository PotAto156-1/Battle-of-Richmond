Confederacy Leader

Edmund Kirby Smith was a skilled Confederate general and West Point graduate who played a major role in the Confederate victory at the Battle of Richmond, Kentucky, in August 1862. Leading the Army of Kentucky, he coordinated a successful attack that crushed Union forces and opened the way for the Confederates to briefly control central Kentucky. His leadership in this battle earned him significant praise and helped boost Southern morale. Later, he commanded the Trans-Mississippi Department, where he kept Confederate forces organized in a remote and challenging region until the war's end. Smith was a smart and effective battlefield leader with strong organizational ability. Leadership Rating: 4/5
